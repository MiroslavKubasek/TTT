# Main idea
Point/Opinion – Reason – Example – Point/Opinion. State your main point/opinion, give a reason, illustrate it, then restate the point/opinion.

## Example 1: Topic "The best advice you ever received"
* **P:** The best advice I ever got: don't be afraid to make mistakes.
* **R:** Because mistakes teach us faster than successes ever could.
* **E:** The first time I spoke on stage I forgot half my notes – and that moment taught me how to think on my feet.
* **P:** That's why I believe mistakes are the best teachers we have.

## Example 2: Topic "Technology you can't imagine life without"
* **P:** The most important piece of technology in my life is a pair of headphones.
* **R:** They give me focus and calm in a noisy world.
* **E:** Thanks to them I can write an essay in a packed café or a rattling train.
* **P:** So headphones are my everyday superpower.

## Example 3: Topic "Why mistakes are worth making"
* **P:** Mistakes are a launchpad for growth.
* **R:** Without them we would stagnate and never learn what actually works.
* **E:** Edison tested thousands of materials before he found the right one for the lightbulb.
* **P:** That's why it's worth failing properly once in a while.
